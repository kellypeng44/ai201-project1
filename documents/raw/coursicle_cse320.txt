CSE 320 - Systems Fundamentals II at Stony Brook University

Credits: 3
Class Size: 30-250 students
Average Professor Rating: 58
Usually Offered: TuTh (1 hour 20 minutes), MW (1 hour 20 minutes)

Description: This course introduces essential concepts of operating systems, compilers, concurrency, and performance analysis, focused around several cross-cutting examples, such as memory management, error handling, and threaded programming. Students examine operating systems from an application programmer's perspective, emphasizing OS APIs. A companion course, CSE 306, covers OS from the kernel implementer's viewpoint. Enrollment Requirements: Prerequisites: C or higher in CSE 220; CSE major.
